SQL vs. NonSQL


The Open Source movement brought to light some thing called NonRel data management system. Yes, after 30 years of an empire SQL for the first time in 1998 met a friend that started to run the race that they though nobody was running. In 2009 the, now No SQL movement got bigger, this time in hands of the people of Last.fm.

But what is all this war about, what is good and bad in SQL. Why NonSQL and what that Rel stand for?

Well the idea its simple but at the same time generates a big difference. Remember that the whole point here is to manage Data. And the amount of Data can be ridiculously massive plus it can create multidimensional monsters that need information to processes it and return more Data in fractions of a second. What is the most efficient way to catch this wave? Well the two boards you can choose from are the relational and the non-relational ones.

Choose between multiple tables organized in a schema or a big base easy to expand horizontally. Well both systems work, each one has better features for some kind of conditions, the other.

Understanding the scope of your program and the scope of each method is the best way to choose smart. Both work, both are great but money it being always going to SQL, therefore more people contributes to libraries and expand it usage in a fast an dynamic pace.
